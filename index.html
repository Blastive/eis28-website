The Tiger

We, the Class of 2028, have unanimously chosen the tiger as our grade animal. A symbol that encompasses the resilience, passion, and determination that define our class in the face of every obstacle. Just as a tiger's unique stripes make it among the most distinct and iconic creatures in the animal kingdom, each individual in our grade brings their own talents and character that contribute to our collective identity. The tiger does not simply endure; it commands respect through strength and grace, qualities we carry with us through every chapter of our journey.

The Blue Hibiscus

To complement the tiger and maxixe blue, the blue hibiscus stands as our grade flower. A rare and extraordinary breed, the blue hibiscus represents the recherché nature of our class — a quality that cannot be easily replicated. It symbolizes the creativity and elegance that distinguish us as individuals, and the quiet confidence with which we present ourselves to the world. Much like the flower itself, the Class of 2028 strives to stand out with grace and leave a lasting impression through our passion, resilience, and unyielding spirit.

Maxixe Blue

Our grade color, maxixe blue, is inspired by the pearlescent glistening of the beryl gemstone — a rare and captivating mineral that symbolizes both ambition and tranquility. The rich and striking hue reflects the unity that binds our class together while representing the relentless determination that continues to propel us forward. It is a color that commands attention without excess, embodying the quiet strength and depth of character that define the Class of 2028.

Together, the tiger, maxixe blue, and blue hibiscus symbolize our class's uniqueness, determination, and passion as the graduating Class of 2028.
